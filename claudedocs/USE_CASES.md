## üåç Domain 1: Translation/Localization Validation

### What It Is
Human verification of translation quality for accuracy, naturalness, and cultural appropriateness. AI can translate, but only humans can judge if it feels natural to native speakers.

### Why Human Workers?
- **Cultural Nuance**: AI misses local expressions, idioms, and cultural context
- **Natural Feel**: Only native speakers can judge if translation sounds natural
- **Context Understanding**: Humans understand tone, formality, and target audience
- **Error Detection**: Catch subtle mistakes AI overlooks

### Real-World Examples

#### Example 1: E-Commerce Localization
**Client**: US-based fashion e-commerce expanding to West Africa  
**Task**: Validate French translations of 50 product descriptions  
**What Workers Do**:
- Read original English and French translation side-by-side
- Rate accuracy (1-5), naturalness (1-5), cultural appropriateness (1-5)
- Flag awkward phrases or cultural mismatches
- Suggest improvements

**Price Range**: $8-15/batch (10-15 products)  
**Time**: 10-20 minutes  
**Worker Requirements**: Native French speaker, basic e-commerce familiarity

## ü§ñ Domain 2: AI Verification & Improvement

### What It Is
Human evaluation of AI-generated content for hallucinations, bias, and cultural appropriateness. Essential for AI companies improving models with human feedback (RLHF - Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback).

### Why Human Workers?
- **Hallucination Detection**: Identify when AI invents false information
- **Bias Recognition**: Spot cultural, gender, or racial biases AI perpetuates
- **Quality Assessment**: Judge coherence, usefulness, and appropriateness
- **Diverse Perspectives**: African viewpoints often missing from AI training

### Real-World Examples

#### Example 1: Chatbot Response Quality Check
**Client**: AI startup building customer service chatbot  
**Task**: Evaluate 20 chatbot responses for quality and appropriateness  
**What Workers Do**:
- Read customer question and AI response
- Rate helpfulness (1-5), accuracy (1-5), tone appropriateness (1-5)
- Flag hallucinations (made-up information)
- Identify bias or inappropriate content
- Suggest improvements

**Price Range**: $5-10/batch (20 responses)  
**Time**: 10-15 minutes  
**Worker Requirements**: Good English comprehension, critical thinking

---

#### Example 2: AI-Generated Content Fact-Check
**Client**: Content platform using AI to generate article summaries  
**Task**: Verify accuracy of AI summaries against original articles  
**What Workers Do**:
- Read original article and AI-generated summary
- Check for factual errors or misrepresentations
- Rate summary quality and completeness
- Flag hallucinations or misleading statements

**Price Range**: $3-8/article review  
**Time**: 5-12 minutes  
**Worker Requirements**: Reading comprehension, attention to detail

---

#### Example 3: Cultural Appropriateness for Global AI
**Client**: Large tech company testing AI chatbot for African markets  
**Task**: Evaluate if AI responses are culturally appropriate for African users  
**What Workers Do**:
- Read AI responses to various questions
- Rate cultural sensitivity and appropriateness
- Flag offensive, insensitive, or confusing content
- Suggest culturally relevant improvements

**Price Range**: $8-15/evaluation session (30 responses)  
**Time**: 15-20 minutes  
**Worker Requirements**: Cultural awareness, AI literacy

## üìç Domain 3: Physical Data Collection & Verification

### What It Is
On-site data collection requiring physical presence: competitor pricing, store audits, infrastructure verification, real-world conditions. AI can't visit stores or take real photos.

### Why Human Workers?
- **Physical Presence Required**: Must be on-location
- **Real-World Verification**: Confirm actual conditions vs online data
- **Photo Evidence**: Authentic photos with GPS verification
- **Local Access**: Knowledge of where to find specific locations

### Real-World Examples

#### Example 1: Competitor Pricing Research
**Client**: Retail chain planning African expansion  
**Task**: Collect competitor prices for 20 common products in Accra  
**What Workers Do**:
- Visit 3-5 specified stores

- GPS verification of location

**Price Range**: $10-20/store visit  
**Time**: 30-45 minutes (including travel)  
**Worker Requirements**: Smartphone, transportation, photography skills

## üì± Domain 4: Mobile App & Game Testing (NEW)

### What It Is
Real-user testing of mobile apps and games for usability, bugs, and market fit. Test on actual devices used by target audiences, especially low-spec Android phones prevalent in emerging markets.

### Why Human Workers?
- **Real User Experience**: AI can't "feel" app usability or frustration
- **Device Diversity**: Test on actual $50-150 Android phones users own
- **Cultural Context**: Emerging market perspective Western teams lack
- **Intuitive Feedback**: Catch UX issues developers overlook

### Real-World Examples

#### Example 1: First Impression Testing
**Client**: Indie game developer launching in African markets  
**Task**: Get first impressions from 10 African users  
**What Workers Do**:
- Download and open app
- Use app freely for 5 minutes
- Rate first impression, ease of use, appeal
- Describe what's confusing or impressive
- Take 2-3 screenshots of key screens

**Price Range**: $3-5/tester

**Worker Requirements**: Smartphone, any skill level
